# MCQ Bank: G7 U.S. Simulator Set

Units: 19-25
Use: End-of-group checkpoint after Driving-Tests.org simulator clips 1-7.

Pass target: 80% or better. Review every miss by error category.

## Q1. When to click

You are watching a hazard-perception simulator clip. A road user is visible near the edge of the scene but has not yet started moving into your path. When should you click?

A. As soon as the road user first appears anywhere on screen
B. Only when the road user begins to create a developing hazard for your path
C. Repeatedly every few seconds until something happens
D. At the end of the clip after confirming what happened

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The click should mark the moment a possible hazard becomes relevant to your driving path, not every visible object.
**Error category:** Timing

## Q2. Missed occlusion cue

You miss a simulator hazard because a vehicle emerged from behind a parked vehicle. What is the best debrief note?

A. I need to memorize this exact clip
B. I missed an occlusion cue and should scan blocked sightlines earlier
C. The simulator was unfair because the hazard was not visible at first
D. I should click every time I see a parked vehicle

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The useful lesson is that parked or stopped vehicles can hide road users. The next attempt should focus on recognizing blocked sightlines earlier.
**Error category:** Occlusion

## Q3. Pattern clicking

During a simulator attempt, you click whenever you see any pedestrian, cyclist, driveway, parked car, or intersection. Your score does not improve. What is the likely problem?

A. You are pattern clicking instead of identifying developing hazards
B. You are waiting too long every time
C. You are not clicking enough objects
D. You should ignore pedestrians unless they enter the lane

**Answer:** A
**Explanation:** Pattern clicking creates noise. A good click is tied to a change in risk: movement, intent, conflict path, reduced space, or blocked view becoming relevant.
**Error category:** Pattern clicking

## Q4. Over-focus

A vehicle ahead slows slightly near a side street. You focus on it so closely that you miss a pedestrian appearing from the opposite side. What should you change on retry?

A. Keep staring at the first vehicle until it fully clears
B. Widen your scan and keep checking the full conflict area
C. Click continuously while focused on the first vehicle
D. Ignore slowing vehicles because pedestrians are more important

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** A visible cue can matter, but over-focusing on one object can cause you to miss a second developing hazard.
**Error category:** Over-focus

## Q5. Late click

You clicked late because you waited until the hazard was directly in front of the vehicle. Which retry strategy is best?

A. Click much earlier whenever any object appears
B. Identify the first moment the road user's intent affects your path
C. Skip the clip and move on without review
D. Click only after the simulator confirms the correct answer

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The goal is to find the start of the developing hazard window. Waiting until the conflict is obvious usually means the response is late.
**Error category:** Late timing

## Q6. Useful miss classification

After a missed simulator hazard, you want to classify the error. Which note is most useful?

A. Bad clip
B. Missed it
C. Occlusion: parked vehicles blocked the view until the road user emerged
D. I need to react faster

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:** A useful debrief names the cause of the miss. Specific categories make practice transferable across clips.
**Error category:** Error classification

## Q7. Shrinking space

In a simulator clip, you notice your lane position is tight near parked vehicles and an oncoming vehicle reduces available space. What should you recognize?

A. A space-management hazard may be developing even without sudden movement
B. There is no hazard unless someone steps into the road
C. You should click only for pedestrians and cyclists
D. The only important cue is the speed limit

**Answer:** A
**Explanation:** A developing hazard can come from shrinking space, not just from a person or vehicle suddenly entering the lane.
**Error category:** Space cushion

## Q8. Transfer to real driving

You finish the seven simulator clips and want to transfer the practice to real driving. Which habit is most useful?

A. Click mentally whenever any object appears
B. Scan for hidden space, intent cues, changing speed, and shrinking space before a conflict becomes urgent
C. Wait until a hazard is directly in front of you before reacting
D. Treat simulator clips as a game unrelated to real driving

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Simulator practice should build real-world anticipation: reading cues early, preparing smoothly, and avoiding late reactions.
**Error category:** Real-driving transfer
