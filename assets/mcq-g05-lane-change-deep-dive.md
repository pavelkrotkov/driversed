## Q1. Lane-change sequence

You are preparing to move one lane left on a multi-lane road. Traffic ahead is steady, and the left lane appears open. What is the best sequence before changing lanes?

A. Check the mirror, move left, then signal once you are halfway over
B. Look over your shoulder first, then signal only if another driver is nearby
C. Speed up, signal, and move left before the gap closes
D. Signal, check mirrors, quick over-the-shoulder check, then go only if clear

**Answer:** D
**Explanation:** SMOG means Signal, Mirror, Over the shoulder, Go. The signal communicates intent, but mirrors and a blind spot check still decide whether the move is safe.
**Error category:** SMOG sequence

## Q2. Signal is not priority

You turn on your left signal to change lanes. A driver in the left lane is slightly behind you but still far enough back that the move might work. What should you do next?

A. Move left immediately because your signal gives you priority
B. Cancel the signal because signaling might make the other driver speed up
C. Keep signaling while checking mirrors, blind spot, and whether the gap stays stable
D. Brake hard so the driver in the left lane has to pass you

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:** The signal is a warning and request for attention, not permission to move. You still need to confirm the space beside and behind you before going.
**Error category:** Signal timing

## Q3. Missing blind-spot confirmation

Your left mirror shows no vehicle next to you, and the lane beside you looks open. What is the missing check before you begin moving left?

A. A quick chin-to-shoulder check into the left blind spot
B. A long look over your shoulder until the lane change is complete
C. A check of the right mirror because you are moving left
D. A honk to warn drivers that you are entering the lane

**Answer:** A
**Explanation:** Mirrors do not prove the blind spot is empty. A quick chin-to-shoulder check confirms the hidden side zone without taking your eyes away from the road for too long.
**Error category:** Blind spot check

## Q4. Staring over the shoulder

During a left lane change, you look over your shoulder for several seconds and the car starts drifting before you are ready. What should you change?

A. Turn your whole body farther so you can see more of the left lane
B. Use a quick chin-to-shoulder glance, then bring your eyes forward before easing over
C. Keep both hands rigid and stare over your shoulder until the lane is clear
D. Skip the shoulder check and trust the mirror instead

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The shoulder check should be quick. Staring over the shoulder can pull your arms and steering, and it also keeps you from watching what is happening ahead.
**Error category:** Overlong shoulder check

## Q5. Gap stability

You see a gap in the left lane, but the car behind that gap is slowly gaining on you. The lane is technically open right now. What is the safest decision?

A. Start moving left because the gap is open at this moment
B. Move left faster so you finish before the other car arrives
C. Signal longer, which makes the other driver responsible for slowing down
D. Wait, because the gap may close before your lane change is complete

**Answer:** D
**Explanation:** A safe lane change needs a stable gap, not just a clear snapshot. If another vehicle is closing, the space may disappear while you are moving over.
**Error category:** Gap stability

## Q6. Closing speed

A car in the left mirror looks small and far away, but each time you check, it appears noticeably closer. What is the main hazard?

A. The car is probably parked on the shoulder
B. The car is far away, so it cannot affect your decision
C. The car's closing speed may make the lane change unsafe
D. The mirror is unreliable, so only the blind spot matters

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:** Distance alone is not enough. If a vehicle is approaching quickly, it can reach your target gap before you complete a smooth lane change.
**Error category:** Closing speed

## Q7. Smooth movement

You have signaled, checked your mirrors, checked your blind spot, and confirmed a stable gap. How should you move into the left lane?

A. Ease over smoothly across several seconds while continuing to monitor traffic
B. Make one quick steering input so you spend less time between lanes
C. Drift left without steering so the move feels natural
D. Brake while moving left so vehicles behind you have more time

**Answer:** A
**Explanation:** A lane change should be controlled and gradual. Smooth movement over several seconds gives you time to track the gap and avoid abrupt steering.
**Error category:** Smooth lane movement

## Q8. Abort option

You begin a left lane change after checking that the gap is open. Halfway through, a vehicle in the left lane speeds up and the space starts closing. What should you do?

A. Continue because you already started the lane change
B. Abort the lane change if it is safer to return to your original lane
C. Accelerate sharply and force your way into the lane
D. Stop between the two lanes until the other driver passes

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Starting a lane change does not mean you must finish it. A good lane-change plan includes an abort option if the gap changes.
**Error category:** Abort decision
