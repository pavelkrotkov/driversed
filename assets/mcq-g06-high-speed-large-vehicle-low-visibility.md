# MCQ Bank: G6 High-Speed, Large-Vehicle, Low-Visibility Driving

Units: 15-18
Use: End-of-group checkpoint after freeway lane changes, freeway and canyon right-of-way, large vehicles, and night/adverse conditions.

Pass target: 80% or better. Review every miss by error category.

## Q1. Freeway merge gap

You are on a freeway on-ramp. Traffic in the right lane is moving faster than you, and a white van is closing from behind in the lane you need. What is the safest decision?

A. Merge immediately because ramp traffic has priority
B. Slow sharply at the end of the ramp and wait for traffic to clear
C. Signal, accelerate to match traffic, check mirrors and blind spot, and use a gap that will not force others to brake
D. Move halfway into the lane so traffic will make space

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:** Freeway traffic already has the right-of-way. A safe merge depends on matching speed, checking mirrors and blind spots, and entering only when the gap is stable.
**Error category:** Freeway merge gap

## Q2. Two-lane-over movement

You want to change lanes on the freeway. The lane beside you looks open, but a car two lanes over is drifting toward the same space. What should you do?

A. Change lanes because the lane directly beside you is clear
B. Check several lanes across, hold your lane, and wait until the shared gap is clearly stable
C. Speed up and claim the space before the other car gets there
D. Signal longer, then move over without another shoulder check

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** At freeway speed, the hazard may come from more than one lane away. Blind spot checks should include the adjacent lane and vehicles that could move into the same gap.
**Error category:** Lane-change blind zone

## Q3. Lane-change commitment

You signaled for a lane change, checked your mirror, and started to move left. Suddenly the lead vehicle in your current lane brakes. What matters most before you continue?

A. Whether your signal has been on for 4 to 5 seconds
B. Whether the lane you are entering has a higher speed limit
C. Whether you still have forward space, a clear blind spot, and room to abort
D. Whether the driver behind you understands your plan

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:** A lane change is not safe just because the signal is on. You need a clear gap, blind spot confirmation, forward space, and an escape option.
**Error category:** Lane-change commitment

## Q4. Being passed

A faster vehicle begins passing you on the left. You are already near the speed limit and your lane is clear. What should you do?

A. Speed up so the passing driver cannot cut in front of you
B. Maintain a predictable path and yield the space needed for the pass to complete safely
C. Drift left slightly to remind the driver you are there
D. Brake hard as soon as the vehicle reaches your rear quarter

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** When being passed, the safest action is to be predictable and allow the pass to finish. Competing for the space increases conflict at high speed.
**Error category:** Being passed

## Q5. Canyon sightline

You are driving on a narrow canyon road. A sharp bend ahead hides the next stretch of road, and there is no shoulder. What is the best hazard-perception response?

A. Keep your speed because you have not seen another vehicle yet
B. Move toward the centerline to see around the curve earlier
C. Slow before the bend, stay in your lane, and prepare for hidden oncoming traffic or stopped traffic
D. Tap the brakes only after you see a vehicle coming toward you

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:** On canyon roads, the bend itself is the hazard because it hides what matters. Speed and lane position should be adjusted before the hidden area appears.
**Error category:** Canyon sightline

## Q6. Canyon turnout

You are driving below the speed limit on a canyon road because conditions are difficult. Five vehicles have formed a line behind you, and a turnout appears ahead. What should you do?

A. Pull into the turnout when safe, let traffic pass, then check carefully before re-entering
B. Keep going because slower traffic always has priority on narrow roads
C. Stop in the travel lane and wave the other cars around
D. Speed up through the next curves so the line behind you clears

**Answer:** A
**Explanation:** A slow vehicle with five or more vehicles behind it should use a turnout when safe. Re-entering requires a fresh signal, mirror check, shoulder check, and safe gap.
**Error category:** Canyon turnout

## Q7. Truck No-Zone

You are driving beside a large truck on the freeway. You cannot see the truck driver's face in the truck's side mirror. What does that mean?

A. The truck driver can still see you because the truck is higher
B. You are likely in a No-Zone and should either pass efficiently or drop back
C. You should stay there because trucks move slowly and predictably
D. You should honk so the truck driver knows you are beside them

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** If you cannot see the driver's face in the mirror, the driver likely cannot see you. Lingering beside a truck leaves you exposed if it moves, drifts, or is affected by wind.
**Error category:** Truck blind zone

## Q8. Truck wide turn

A large truck ahead signals right but swings left before the turn. There is space between the truck and the curb. What should you do?

A. Drive into the open space before the truck completes the turn
B. Stay back and give the truck room to complete the wide turn
C. Pass on the right if you can clear the truck before the crosswalk
D. Move close behind the truck so other drivers do not cut in

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Large vehicles need extra turning room and may swing wide. The space beside a turning truck can disappear quickly.
**Error category:** Truck turning room

## Q9. Night glare

You are driving at night on a two-lane road. Oncoming headlights are causing glare, and the lane edge is harder to read. What is the safest adjustment?

A. Stare directly at the oncoming headlights until the car passes
B. Increase speed so you spend less time in the glare
C. Reduce speed, avoid staring into the glare, and use lane edges and shoulder movement to guide your search
D. Turn on high beams while the other car is approaching

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:** Glare reduces night vision and search quality. Slowing and scanning road edges and shoulders gives more time to detect curves, pedestrians, animals, or stopped vehicles.
**Error category:** Night glare

## Q10. Wet low-visibility freeway

It is raining on the freeway. Spray from traffic reduces visibility, and the road surface is wet. What should change first?

A. Follow closer so other vehicles block the spray
B. Keep normal speed but brake harder if traffic slows
C. Increase following distance, reduce speed early, and make steering and braking inputs gentler
D. Move into the fastest lane because it usually has fewer trucks

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:** Wet and low-visibility conditions reduce both traction and seeing distance. The same hazards require earlier speed reduction, longer space cushions, and smoother control.
**Error category:** Wet low-visibility adjustment
