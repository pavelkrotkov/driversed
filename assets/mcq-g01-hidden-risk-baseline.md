# MCQ Bank: G1 Hidden-Risk Baseline

Units: 1-2
Use: End-of-group checkpoint after Toyota Risk-ATTEND first pass and replay.

Pass target: 80% or better. Review every miss by error category.

## Q1. Parked van at a crosswalk

You are driving on a residential street. A parked van sits close to a crosswalk, blocking your view of the curb and sidewalk behind it. Nothing is moving yet. What is the best hazard-perception response?

A. Ignore it until a pedestrian actually steps out
B. Treat the van as a visible object that creates hidden space
C. Move into the oncoming lane immediately to get a better view
D. Speed up slightly so you spend less time beside the van

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** The van itself is not the only issue. It blocks sightlines, so a pedestrian, cyclist, or child could appear from the hidden space around it.
**Error category:** Hidden-space recognition

## Q2. Hedge-blocked driveway

You are approaching a driveway bordered by tall hedges. You cannot see whether a vehicle, cyclist, or pedestrian is about to exit. What should you predict?

A. Nothing is there unless you can see it
B. The hedge is the hazard only if it touches the road
C. A hidden actor could emerge from the driveway or sidewalk gap
D. The safest move is to brake hard before every driveway

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:** A blocked driveway is a hidden-risk zone. The correct prediction is not that danger is certain, but that someone could emerge with little warning.
**Error category:** Driveway occlusion

## Q3. Pause-and-predict before a curve

During a pause-and-predict exercise, the video is paused before a bend with parked cars near the outside of the curve. What is the most useful prediction?

A. The road will stay clear because no hazard is visible yet
B. A hidden road user or vehicle may appear where the curve opens up
C. The main risk is that your speedometer may be hard to read
D. You should focus only on the center of your lane

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Curves reduce the view ahead. Parked cars near the bend add more blocked sightlines, so the next risk may appear only after the road opens.
**Error category:** Curve sightline prediction

## Q4. Stopped delivery truck

You see a delivery truck stopped at the curb. Its rear doors are closed, and no one is visible. What is the best first gentle action?

A. Ease off the accelerator and prepare to cover the brake
B. Honk before passing every stopped truck
C. Steer sharply around it without checking mirrors
D. Maintain speed because the truck is not moving

**Answer:** A
**Explanation:** The first response to hidden risk should usually be gentle and early: reduce speed slightly, increase readiness, and create time to react.
**Error category:** First gentle action

## Q5. Gaps between parked cars

A row of parked cars lines the right side of the road. You notice gaps between two cars near a school zone. What should you be most alert for?

A. A hidden pedestrian or child stepping through the gap
B. A tire mark on the road surface
C. Whether all parked cars are the same color
D. The exact model of each parked car

**Answer:** A
**Explanation:** Gaps between parked cars can hide people until they are close to the travel lane. The important cue is the hidden space, not the parked cars as objects.
**Error category:** Parked-vehicle occlusion

## Q6. Replay reflection

You are replaying a clip after your first pass. On the first viewing, you noticed a parked SUV but did not slow mentally or physically. On replay, what should you ask yourself at that moment?

A. What hidden space does this visible object create?
B. How new is the SUV?
C. Would the SUV driver be legally responsible for everything?
D. Can I memorize the exact second when the hazard appears?

**Answer:** A
**Explanation:** Replay is for improving prediction. The key habit is linking visible blockers to possible hidden actors before they appear.
**Error category:** Pause-and-predict reflection

## Q7. Bus blocking a crosswalk

A bus is stopped ahead near an intersection. You cannot see the crosswalk on the far side of the bus. What is the safest interpretation?

A. The bus is large, so it protects you from pedestrians
B. A pedestrian may be hidden by the bus and enter your path
C. The only hazard is traffic behind you
D. You should accelerate before the bus pulls out

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Large vehicles create major blind zones. The risk is not just the bus moving, but people or vehicles hidden by it.
**Error category:** Large-vehicle occlusion

## Q8. Baseline hidden-risk thinking

You are driving past a residential block with parked cars, hedges, and several driveways. Nothing has moved into your lane. Which response best shows hidden-risk baseline thinking?

A. Keep your eyes fixed straight ahead because the lane is clear
B. Scan for blocked sightlines, ease speed, and prepare for a possible emergence
C. Brake hard at every parked car so no prediction is needed
D. Wait until a hazard appears, then make one sudden correction

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:** Hidden-risk driving means identifying where hazards could emerge before they become obvious. The goal is early, smooth preparation rather than late reaction.
**Error category:** Baseline hidden-risk scanning
